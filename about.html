Ice Kachang

Ice kacang is a Singaporean dessert that is also popular in Malaysia and Brunei. Literally meaning 'bean ice', it was originally made of only shaved ice and red beans. The number and diversity of ingredients has since expanded to also include sweet corn, grass jelly, roasted peanuts and cubes of jelly as common ingredients.




Carrot Cake

Not to be confused with the dessert carrot cake, this savoury carrot cake has no carrot, at least not of the orange variety. Instead, the core ingredients of the cake are rice flour and white radish, which some call white carrot. The mixture is steamed, then cut into cubes and fried with garlic, eggs and preserved radish. These smooth and soft fried rice cakes can be found in almost every hawker centre in Singapore. It is served black (fried with sweet dark soya sauce) or white (original).




Popiah

Popiah is a spring roll originating from the Fujian province in China. It comprises a soft, thin paper-like crepe and stir-fried turnip, cooked with other ingredients such as bean sprouts, lettuce leafs, grated carrots, slices of Chinese sausage, thinly sliced fried tofu, chopped peanuts, fried shallots, and shredded omelette. It is eaten in accompaniment with a sweet bean sauce and/or chili.
